That "toggle" trick essentially forced the **plasma-shell** to restart its indexing of the directory. It’s the Linux equivalent of "unplugging it and plugging it back in," and you’re right—it shouldn't be necessary in a stable environment.

The "fragility" you're experiencing usually comes down to **config drift**. When you have multiple Desktop Environments (LXQt and KDE) installed, they sometimes fight over who controls the `user-dirs.dirs` file.

Here is how to "lock it down" so Plasma stops losing your files:

### 1. Hard-Code the Desktop Path
Plasma might be losing track of where your desktop actually is. Let’s make sure it’s hard-coded correctly.
* Open the terminal and run: `nano ~/.config/user-dirs.dirs`
* Look for the line: `XDG_DESKTOP_DIR="$HOME/Desktop"`
* If it says anything else (like `"$HOME/"` or `"$HOME/Public"`), change it back to `"$HOME/Desktop"`.
* Save and exit (**Ctrl+O**, **Enter**, **Ctrl+X**).

### 2. Disable "Icon Sorting" Automation
Sometimes Plasma tries to be "helpful" by grouping or sorting icons in a way that makes them disappear off-screen or into a scrollable area.
* Right-click Desktop -> **Icons**.
* Ensure **Sorting** is set to "Manual" or "Name" and **uncheck** "Lock in place" for a moment to see if things rearrange themselves.

### 3. Check for "Invisible" Widgets
Since Plasma treats the desktop as a canvas, it's possible you accidentally created a **transparent widget** that is sitting *over* your icons, blocking clicks or visibility.
* Press `Alt+D, Alt+L` to unlock widgets.
* See if there are any empty rectangular outlines on your screen. If so, delete them.

### 4. The "Nuclear" Prevention (The Plasma Way)
If you want to keep using Plasma but hate its "smart" features, you can lock the desktop configuration file so it literally *cannot* change itself:
```bash
chmod 444 ~/.config/plasma-org.kde.plasma.desktop-appletsrc
```
> **Warning:** This makes your desktop settings "Read-Only." You won't be able to change your wallpaper or move icons permanently until you change it back to `644`.
