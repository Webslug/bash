Since you asked, here is the "secret level" of `~/.inputrc` configuration!

We can’t easily make a "smart" button that knows exactly where quotes are, but we can create a **"Delete to End of Line"** shortcut that is much easier to hit than the default `Ctrl+K`, or a **"Jump back to the last Quote"** shortcut.

However, the most efficient way to handle those massive Lorem Ipsum blocks is actually to use **"Backward Kill Line."**

### Option 1: The "Kill Everything Behind Me" (Super Fast)
If you are at the very end of that massive command and want to delete the whole quote block instantly:
1. Tap **Alt+B** a few times to jump the cursor to the start of the quote.
2. Use a shortcut to kill everything from the cursor to the end.

Add this to your `~/.inputrc`:
```text
# Map Ctrl+G to delete everything from cursor to the END of the line
"\C-g": kill-line
```
*Now, you just move the cursor to the start of the quote and hit **Ctrl+G**. Poof, it’s gone.*

---

### Option 2: The "Jump to Quote" Shortcut
If you want to skip the arrow keys entirely, we can tell Readline to "Search backward for the `"` character."

Add this to your `~/.inputrc`:
```text
# Press Ctrl+X then " to jump back to the previous double quote
"\C-x\"": backward-char-search
```
*To use this: Press **Ctrl+X**, then type **"**. Your cursor will teleport backward to the last quote it finds.*

---

### Option 3: The "Bash Professional" Way (No Config Needed)
If you don't want to mess with more config files, there is a built-in trick called **Unix Word Rubout**.

Press **Ctrl+Alt+H** (or **Ctrl+W** in some setups).
* Usually, `Ctrl+W` stops at **spaces**.
* But if you use **`Ctrl+Alt+?`** (Backspace), it often deletes until it hits a "special" character like a quote.

---

### My Recommendation for your `~/.inputrc`
To make your life as easy as "Kim" deserves, I'd go with the **Kill Line** re-map. Open your `~/.inputrc` again and make it look like this:

```text
# Existing settings
set enable-bracketed-paste off
set completion-ignore-case on

# The "Big Delete" - kills everything to the right of the cursor
# I chose Ctrl+K (default) but you can ensure it's active
"\C-k": kill-line

# The "Big Jump" - jump backward to the start of the previous word 
# (Hold Ctrl and hit Left Arrow)
"\e[1;5D": backward-word
# (Hold Ctrl and hit Right Arrow)
"\e[1;5C": forward-word
```

### How to use it on your `python3 say.py "Lorem..."` command:
1. Press **Up Arrow** to get the command back.
2. Hold **Ctrl** and tap **Left Arrow** once or twice. Your cursor will "hop" over the giant block of text to the beginning of the quotes.
3. Hit **Ctrl+K**. The entire quote is deleted in one frame.
