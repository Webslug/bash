To answer your question: Yes, there are a few "shortcuts" for deleting that massive block of text at the end of a command. Since Readline (which you just configured) handles your keyboard input, you have some powerful "Emacs-style" shortcuts built right in.

### 1. The "Kill Word" Shortcut (`Alt + Backspace` or `Ctrl + W`)
This is usually the fastest way to delete "chunks" of a command.
* **`Ctrl + W`**: Deletes the "word" behind the cursor (it stops at the first space it hits).
* **`Alt + Backspace`**: Similar to `Ctrl + W`, but it’s often "smarter" about punctuation depending on your settings.

**The Catch:** If your quote is one giant block with no spaces, one `Ctrl + W` kills the whole thing. If it has spaces (like your Lorem Ipsum example), you'd have to mash it repeatedly.

### 2. The "Kill to End of Line" (`Ctrl + K`)
This is the "sniper" approach.
1.  Press **Up Arrow** to bring back the long command.
2.  Use **Left Arrow** (or `Alt + B` to jump back by words) to move your cursor to just before the first quotation mark.
3.  Press **`Ctrl + K`**.
4.  Everything from the cursor to the very end of the line vanishes instantly.

### 3. The "Kill to Start of Line" (`Ctrl + U`)
If you find yourself at the very end of that massive paragraph:
1.  Press **`Ctrl + U`**.
2.  **Warning:** This deletes the *entire* line. You'd have to re-type `python3 say.py`. 

---

### Pro-Tip: Jumping the Cursor
Since your Lorem Ipsum text is huge, don't use the arrow keys to move the cursor—it'll take forever. Use these instead:
* **`Ctrl + A`**: Jump to the **beginning** of the line.
* **`Ctrl + E`**: Jump to the **end** of the line.
* **`Alt + B`**: Jump **backward** one word.
* **`Alt + F`**: Jump **forward** one word.

### Summary Table of Shortcuts
| Action | Shortcut |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Delete to end of line** | `Ctrl + K` |
| **Delete back one word** | `Ctrl + W` |
| **Jump to start of line** | `Ctrl + A` |
| **Jump to end of line** | `Ctrl + E` |
